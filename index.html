Deck Tier Grades

Your average 5-Finger score determines your deck's tier. Here's what each level means. Climb the ladder toward inevitability.

Inevitability Machine

4.6–5.0 average score

The deck plays itself. Every card is load-bearing AND replaceable. The synergy web is so dense that any opening hand leads to the same destination. Removal doesn't hurt you because redundancy is built into every seam.

Engine Online

3.6–4.5 average score

The deck hums. Cards trigger other cards in clean chains. Removing one piece doesn't collapse the whole thing because there's redundancy built in. You can mulligan and still function. This is the gold standard.

Focused

2.6–3.5 average score

Every card knows its job. The engine has moving parts that work together. Cuts are hard because everything matters. You have a cohesive strategy, but there's room for polish and more bridge cards to smooth the edges.

Standard

1.6–2.5 average score

A deck with a theme but loose connections. You have a plan, but half your cards don't know what it is. Most precons live here. There's potential, but it needs tightening — more signal cards, fewer utility slots.

Synergy Soup

≤1.5 average score

Cards that happen to be in the same sleeve. No cohesion, no engine, no plan. You're playing 99 individual cards, not a deck. Not bad — just unfocused. The framework will show you exactly which cards to cut to climb the ladder.

How It Works

Six steps. Most of it is auto-filled. You review, adjust, and walk away with a full diagnostic.

1

Import Your Deck

Paste your deck list from Archidekt, Moxfield, or any standard format. Enter your commander name. The tool fetches every card's data from Scryfall automatically — oracle text, mana value, types, everything.

2

Fill the Character Sheet

Define your commander's role. Is it an Enabler (engine for the 99) or a Payoff (the win condition itself)? What's the primary ability? The tool suggests 5 secondary ability categories by analyzing your cards' mechanical keywords.

3

Categorize Every Card

Each card gets a primary and optional secondary category, auto-filled from oracle text analysis. You review and adjust. A live dashboard shows your ratios against framework targets (18 Velocity, 12-15 Ramp, 9-15 Interaction, 33-36 Lands).

4

5-Finger Quick Check

Score every non-land card on 5 dimensions: Commander Signal, Dual-Category, Web Connected, Independent Value, and Temporal Fit. Each card gets a 0-5 score. Low-scoring cards are your cut candidates.

5

Results & SWP Score

Your deck's Synergy Web Protocol profile: Signal Density, Commander Independence, and Cross-Category Pollination — each graded F through S. Plus category ratio analysis, Pillar of Failure detection, and your bottom-10 cut list.

6

Export & Feedback

Download your full audit as a text report. Share feedback on every step — what worked, what was confusing, what needs improvement. Your input directly shapes the next version.

The Synergy Web Protocol

Most "synergy" metrics measure popularity. The SWP measures how your cards actually connect to each other. It treats your deck as a network — each card is a node, each interaction is an edge.

SD

Signal Density

What percentage of your non-land cards directly interact with your commander's primary ability? This separates focused decks from "goodstuff piles." A Sol Ring is great — but it doesn't interact with your commander's text box.

Target: 50-80% for most strategies
CI

Commander Independence

If your commander gets exiled permanently, how much of your deck still functions? Low CI means you fall apart without your commander — the "Top-Down Trap." High CI means you can grind even without the engine online.

Target: 65-95% for Enabler commanders
CCP

Cross-Category Pollination

How many cards serve double duty — a ramp piece that also triggers your secondary ability, an interaction spell that feeds your engine. Bridge cards are the connective tissue that makes a deck feel "greater than the sum of its parts."

Target: 30-60% is the sweet spot
ED + RI

Engine Depth & Resilience

Engine Depth measures how long your synergy chains run — one card triggers another triggers another. Resilience Index asks: if you remove the single most-connected card, how much of the web survives? Together they measure engine quality and redundancy.

The SWP gives you a letter grade (F through S) on each dimension, plus a composite Synergy Engine Score. It's not about having "good cards" — it's about having cards that talk to each other.

Why This Exists

EDHREC tells you what cards are popular. The 8x8 method tells you to run 8 categories of 8 cards. Neither tells you whether your cards actually work together.

Manabot Insights splits your 99 into strict category ratios backed by hypergeometric probability. The Synergy Web Protocol scores every card on how it connects to the rest of the deck. Together, they give you a complete structural diagnosis — not opinions, math.

This is a beta. The methodology is being tested and refined with real decks from real players. Your feedback shapes the next version.
